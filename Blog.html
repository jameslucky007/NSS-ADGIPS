The Rakshin Project: Raising Awareness on Child Abuse and the POCSO Act

On September 24, 2024, the Rakshin Project conducted an impactful awareness event at the ADGIPS Auditorium, where over 210 participants gathered to discuss a pressing social issue—child abuse and the POCSO (Protection of Children from Sexual Offences) Act. The Events Objective The event aimed to educate students and faculty about child sexual abuse (CSA), its prevalence, and the legal framework under the POCSO Act, which is designed to protect minors from sexual exploitation. The Rakshin Project, an initiative by Sakshi NGO, has been actively working towards preventing child sexual abuse and gender-based violence through youth-led movements, legal awareness, and sustained engagement across educational institutions ￼ ￼. Chief Guests and Their Contributions 1. Ms. Paavni Sharma (Member, Sakshi NGO) • A dedicated child rights activist, Ms. Paavni Sharma has been tirelessly working to combat child sexual abuse and promote gender equality. • As a key member of Sakshi NGO, she has played a vital role in conducting workshops, legal awareness drives, and survivor support initiatives. 2. Mr. Vaibhav Goyal (Workshop Coordinator, Rakshin Project) • With a passion for youth empowerment and education, Mr. Vaibhav Goyal has been instrumental in organizing workshops and awareness campaigns across institutions. • His expertise in interactive training methods and legal education has helped young individuals understand the importance of early intervention and reporting mechanisms for child abuse cases. Highlights of the Event 1. Understanding the Issue • Speakers highlighted the alarming statistics on child abuse. 2. Legal Awareness: The POCSO Act • The POCSO Act was explained in detail, including its provisions for reporting abuse, legal penalties, and the responsibilities of institutions in safeguarding children ￼. • Real-life case studies were shared to illustrate how the law is implemented and the challenges faced in achieving justice for survivors. 3. Empowering Participants as Rakshins • The Rakshin Project encourages young individuals to become Rakshins—preventers of harm who can intervene and educate their communities on child safety and abuse prevention. Impact and Takeaways The interactive session enabled participants to engage in meaningful discussions, share perspectives, and commit to spreading awareness within their circles. The event reinforced that child protection is a shared responsibility, and legal awareness is a crucial step in prevention ￼. By conducting such events, the Rakshin Project continues to build a network of responsible youth leaders who can contribute to a safer, more informed society.
